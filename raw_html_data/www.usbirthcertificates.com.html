About U.S. Birth Certificates

A birth certificate is a legal document issued by a vital record office. It is used as proof of identity and when born in the United States it establishes eligibility for American citizenship. You can use a certified copy of your birth records to apply for:

  • a driver’s license
  • a passport
  • government benefits
  • social security
  • enrolling in school/university

Birth certificates in the U.S. include personal identifiable information, like name, date and place where the birth occurred, sex and the names of the parents.
